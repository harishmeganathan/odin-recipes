Chicken 65

Description:

Chicken 65 is a hot, deep-fried chicken dish from Chennai, India. It features bite-sized, boneless chicken marinated in spiced yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and red chili powder, then coated with cornflour and deep-fried until crispy and bright red-orange. It's tangy, fiery, and savory — often served with onion rings and lemon wedges. The "65" in its name remains a mystery (possibly from the year 1965 or a 65-day-old chicken).

Ingredients:

  • Boneless chicken - 500g (bite-sized pieces)
  • Yogurt - 1/4 cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tbsp
  • Red chili powder - 1 tbsp
  • Turmeric - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Lemon juice - 1 tbsp
  • Cornflour - 2 tbsp
  • All-purpose flour (or rice flour for extra crispiness) - 1 tbsp
  • Egg - 1 (optional, for binding)
  • Oil - for deep frying
  • Curry leaves - a few (optional)
  • Green chilies - 2 (slit, optional)
  • Onion rings & lemon wedges - for serving

Steps:

  1. Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, salt, and lemon juice in a bowl. Add chicken pieces and coat well. Let it marinate for 30 minutes (or up to 2 hours in the fridge).
  2. Add cornflour and all-purpose flour to the marinated chicken. Mix well. Add egg if using (helps coating stick). The mixture should be thick and coat each piece.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai for deep frying. To check if ready, drop a small piece of batter - it should sizzle and rise immediately.
  4. Drop chicken pieces carefully into hot oil. Fry on medium heat until golden brown and crispy (about 4-5 minutes). Do not overcrowd. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towel.
  5. In a small pan, heat 1 tsp oil. Add curry leaves and slit green chilies. Sauté for 10 seconds, then toss the fried chicken in it.
  6. Garnish with onion rings and serve hot with lemon wedges and a side of mint chutney or ketchup.
